Why Does This Matter?

Gerrymandering affects millions of American citizens, changing the future of the United States.

The core aspect to the politics and elections of the United States is democracy and power in the common man. The representative democracy that the United States uses is completely damaged by gerrymandering. The use of gerrymandering goes against the meaning of democracy and take representation away from the civilians and puts it in the hands of powerful politicians.

When a community is affected by gerrymandering, their population is split. They lose their collective voice in their local and state government, leading to the misrepresentation of their district and the district's needs. Furthermore, gerrymandering is often imposed against minority groups, pushing against centuries of work for civil rights and the foundation of American civil liberties and rights.

  • Gerrymandering is becoming more frequent: In 2024, only 1 in every 10 districts in the country were competitive, much of it being due to gerrymandering. The issue with this loss of competitiveness is that it is unnatural and unfair. Though these districts are safe, they are not representative of the people who live in them. This leads to a loss of democracy through a lack of competition.
  • Gerrymandering is getting worse: With the innovation in technology, especially in recent decades, gerrymanders can now be created with programs, allowing it to be much more precise. Furthermore, a precedent was set in the 2019 case Rucho v. Common Cause where the Supreme Court ruled that gerrymandering was past the reach of federal courts to address. Now that the judicial precedent has been set, it will be much more difficult to fight gerrymandering in court, which has ultimately led to gerrymandering becoming more frequent.
