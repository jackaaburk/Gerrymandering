Gerrymandering

Why This Matters

Every ten years, district lines are redrawn. How those lines are drawn dictates the balance of power in Congress and state legislatures for the next decade. When districts are gerrymandered, elections become uncompetitive, extreme candidates are favored, and millions of votes effectively don't matter.
